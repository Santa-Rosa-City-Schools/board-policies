The Superintendent or designee shall support opportunities for certificated staff to learn both from outside sources and from each other. These opportunities may include, but are not limited to:

1. Visits to other classrooms and other schools to observe and analyze teaching
2. Attendance at professional education conferences or committee meetings
3. Classes/workshops offered by the district, county office of education, state projects, private organizations, or other appropriate agencies
4. Participation in professional development networks that promote inquiry and allow staff to analyze and evaluate each other's work
5. Peer conferences and/or joint staff preparation time
6. Participation in curriculum development projects
7. Participation in educational research or innovation efforts
8. Assistance from or service as a support provider and/or peer coach
9. Discussions and/or collaboration with business and community agencies for the purpose of identifying the skills, knowledge, and aptitudes necessary for specific career paths and developing meaningful career-related, work-based learning experiences
10. Study and research in subject-matter content and effective educational practices
11. Follow-up activities that help staff to implement newly acquired skills

The Superintendent or designee shall approve the participation of individual staff members in district-provided or external staff development activities which may require release time, leave of absence, or other district resources.
