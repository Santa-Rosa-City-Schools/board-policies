In order to meet individual student needs and encourage all students to complete their high school education, the Governing Board desires to provide flexibility in the completion of prescribed courses in accordance with law.

The results from the General Educational Development (GED) test shall not be used as an alternative means of satisfying course requirements (Education Code 51420- 51427)

Students may fulfill any course requirement for graduation through the following:

1. Career technical education classes offered in high school (Education Code 51225.3 )
2. Courses offered by regional occupational centers or programs (Education Code 51225.3 )
3. Independent study (Education Code 51225.3 )
4. Credit earned at a postsecondary institution (Education Code 48800 , 51225.3 )
5. Telecommunication and correspondence instruction from a California university or college accredited for teacher training in accordance with 5 CCR 1633 (Education Code 51740 - 51741 )
6. Successful participation in district interscholastic athletic programs carried on wholly or partially after regular school hours qualifies towards meeting the district's physical education requirement (Education Code 51242 ). A student shall be eligible to satisfy graduation requirements in this manner only if the interscholastic program entails a comparable amount of time and physical activity. If a student has previously completed ten (10) credits towards the PE requirement and upon approval of the school administration, five (5) credits shall be given per season of sport up to a maximum of ten (10) credits for successful participation in interscholastic athletics. Students shall not receive more than a total of ten (10) credits for interscholastic athletics participation. Retroactive credit shall not be granted for participation in interscholastic athletics from the previous year.
7. In accordance with 5 CCR 1632 , credit toward the district's foreign language requirement will be granted for foreign language studies successfully completed in a private school in the following languages: Chinese, French, German, Greek (classical and modern), Hebrew (classical and modern), Italian, Japanese, Jewish, Latin, Spanish, and Russian (Education Code 51243 - 51244 )
