1. Including parents/guardians and community as decisions-makers and encouraging their appropriateleadership in governance, advisory, and advocacy roles.Suggested activities:
? Handbook with rights and responsibilities of participants
? Solicit parents and community representation and input on councils, task forces, ad hoc committees,
etc.
? Include parent and community input
? Provide leadership training for site council members, interested parents, and community members
about rights and responsibilities.
? Post Santa Rosa City School Board agendas and minutes at school sites where they are accessible to
parents and community
? Publicize that Santa Rosa City School Board agendas and minutes are posted at school sites where
they are accessible to parents and community
? Notice to papers regarding Santa Rosa City School Board meetings, places and times
? "Board Highlights" to sites, including feeder schools, to be posted
2. Fostering an environment in each school and each level of schooling that invites and welcomes
parents to be active partners in their children's education.
Suggested activities:
? Communication - fireside chats in homes, informal open office, positive phone calls from principals
? Publicize roles of all parties - teachers, parents, principals, etc.
? Offer orientation by the volunteer coordinator to school volunteers
? Brochure listing ways parents can be involved
? Share definitions of school jargon with parents
? Encourage activities/programs that recognize diverse cultures
? Provide help with transportation needs to parents by providing bus schedules for public
transportation, allowing them to ride school buses or encouraging car pools at school sites
? Write surveys to ask parents input.
Request that parents agree to respond to school survey in their
Family-School Compact.
(see Compact definition under item 4)
? Provide resources for interpretation and translation
? Distribute monthly updated calendars to school site, including feeder schools

? Identify opportunities for all parents to participate
? Conduct thank you and/or welcome get-togethers for volunteers
3. Providing and encouraging programs for parents to develop parenting skills; including skills that
support children's learning both at home and in the classroom.
Suggested activities:
? Family nights dealing with issues such as:
math, tutorial, ESL (English as a Second Language),
citizenship, English, parenting workshops, open gymnasium nights
? Classroom curriculum nights
? Parenting classes and child development days
? Utilize community resources, programs, and services for support to families
? Classroom newsletter
4. Providing support and resources information for school staff and parents to enable them to
implement and sustain effective involvement.
Suggested activities:
? Parent center that includes lending library
? Invite agencies that provide support to families for information nights
? Encourage colleges to include parent involvement in their curriculum for new teacher training and
continuing education programs
? Encourage a Family-School Compact.
A Family-School Compact is:
An agreement between school
and families that describes what schools, families, and students will do to ensure that students will be
successful in achieving the school's learning standards and goals
? Provide training and support for teachers on the use of volunteers
? Provide training for site volunteer coordinator (non-salaried)
5. Providing clear and timely two-way communication with parents.
Providing information regarding
their student's progress and the school's programs.
Suggested activities:
? District provision of a one-page article with Board news and calendar updates to each school site for
inclusion in its own newsletters, including feeder schools
? Offer a minimum of two parent conferences at all grade level (must be negotiated with teachers)
? Master planning calendar to coordinate site and district activities (recommend nights for parent club

meeting:
elementary on Monday, middle school on Tuesday, high school on Thursday, for example,
leaving Wednesday for parents to attend school Board meetings)
? Post Santa Rosa City School Board agendas and minutes at school sites where they are accessible to
parents
? Encourage hot line/web site
? Schools will notify parents as required by Education Code 51550, Article 6 (Sex Education Courses).
Schools will also make an effort to notify parents through the use of curriculum nights, parent
conferences, newsletters, bulletins and grade-level expectancies and district standards
? Classroom calendar, newsletter, and volunteer phone tree
? Have readily available class course descriptions and fees
? Report back on use of in-service days and paid teacher workshops to parents through newsletters
? Copies of district policies should be available upon request at sites
? Involving feeder school parents
? Provide translation as needed *
Dependent on available resources
6. Involving parents and community in tutorial and support roles at the school.
Providing training as
needed for specific jobs.
Suggested activities:
? Solicit and train non-salaried volunteer coordinators to assist in recruiting and supporting parent and
community volunteers at each school site
? More frequent curriculum focus nights
? Provide volunteer job descriptions
? Family-School Compact should include volunteer component.
(see Compact definition under item 4.
7. Helping connect students and families with community resources that provide support and
educational enrichment.
Suggested activities:
? Developed database of community resources of on-site use
? Newsletter articles
? Lending libraries
? Ongoing meeting information and speakers on various topics to parents

? Community resource guides
8. Recognizing that each school serves a community with unique needs.
Individual schools shall
develop ways to meet those needs.
Suggested activities:
? Adopt Parental Involvement Policy at each site and develop an individual implementation plan
? Provide English classes for parents, as well as children as needed
? Provide translation/interpreters as needed
? Provide quality child care as needed
? Provide transportation as needed
? Support student who do not have resources as needed
? Be sensitive to community issues
Dependent on available issues
9. Reaching out to feeder schools so parents of students joining the school district in the seventh grade
will be informed as well.
Suggested activities:
? Solicit feeder school parent representation and input on councils, task forces, ad hoc committees, etc.
? Provide leadership training for interested feeder school parents about rights and responsibilities
? Post agendas and minutes for all meetings at school Board agendas and minutes are posted at school
sites where they are accessible to all parents
? "Board Highlights" posted at sites, encourage feeder schools to post also
? District will provide a one-page article with Board news and calendar updates to each school site for
inclusion in its own newsletters, including feeder schools
? Invite parents from feeder schools to staff development days
? Volunteer recruitment forms
? Inform feeder school parents about the contents of classroom curriculum, grade-level expectancies,
and district standards
10. Developing an implementation plan at each site based on this policy and suggested activities that
will involve parents and community.
Providing an annual evaluation to all parents, teachers, and the
Board of Education.
Provide guidelines for each site to conduct individual activities.
