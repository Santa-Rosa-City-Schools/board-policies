**Fund-Raising**

The Board of Education recognizes the need for students to occasionally conduct fundraising activities in order to provide for the maintenance and enrichment of student activity programs. Such activities should be conducted in a manner which will provide the funding necessary without frequent broad and direct solicitation of the public and community.

**General**

Fund-raising activities shall be designed to keep student/teacher involvement to a minimum.

Fund-raising activities shall not be in conflict with public law or district policy.

Student bulletin boards may be made available for display of posters or other materials relative to walk-a-thons, bike-a-thons, or other promotional activities relative to the fund drives of approved community-oriented organizations. Such posters and/or materials should have the approval of the principal or designee before posting and an announcement should be placed on the bulletin board stating that neither the school nor the district directs or sponsors these activities.

Health-oriented organizations may make presentations to the student body when they are of an educational nature only. These presentations will not include solicitations of parents or staff and must have prior approval of the principal or designee. A request for a presentation may be denied if said presentation is in conflict with or disrupts the educational process.
