**Implementing Parameters**

1. In order to participate in school uniforms/common student dress (CSD), all schools must complete the school process steps and adhere to school guidelines.
2. The school's participation will be approved by the Assistant Superintendent of Secondary or Elementary Curriculum and Instruction.
3. The principal or designee shall give parents/guardians at least six months' notice before a school uniform/common student dress policy is implemented. (Education Code 35183 )
4. The principal must ensure that the parents/guardians, community members, and staff have had sufficient opportunities to study and address all the related issues.
5. Once a school dress code has been approved at the school level, the parents/guardians, the principal, and staff shall jointly select the uniforms/CSD to be worn. (Education Code 35183 )
6. To assist in the planning stages, principals should be given a "Principal Sequence for Common School Dress/Uniform" sampler packet for support and use.
7. The Assistant Superintendent may determine the criteria for student eligibility of financial assistance when purchasing uniforms, as required or necessary.
8. All schools will participate in an evaluation at the end of the first and third years of the program. Parents/guardians, students, and staff will be consulted for the evaluation.

**Guidelines**

1. Both the decision to participate and the choices of uniforms/CSD will be school-based. The district will not mandate uniforms/CSD for all schools.
2. In order for the Board of Education to approve the results of the parent balloting, the school must demonstrate that:
  1. One hundred percent of the eligible families must be provided ballots.
  2. Eighty percent of the eligible families must cast ballots.
  3. Sixty-six percent of those eligible families casting ballots must vote in favor of having uniforms/CSD.
  4. Ballots are tallied by family vote; not by the number of students in each family.
3. Families of K-5 students will vote at their elementary schools when uniforms/CSD are considered.
4. Families of sixth grade students will vote for or against uniforms/CSD when the middle school they will be attending is considering school uniforms/CSD. Middle school principals will work collaboratively with partner elementary principals to insure the participation of sixth grade families.
5. Families of eighth grade students will vote for or against uniforms/CSD when the high school they will be attending is considering school uniforms/CSD. High school principals will work collaboratively with middle school principals to insure the participation of eighth grade families.
6. Schools must provide notice of the availability of resources to assist families who may need financial assistance.
7. Surveys designed for use in determining the interest of staff, parents/guardians, and students for uniforms/CSD must be prepared in the languages appropriate for the school community.
8. Schools will have district support regarding procedures and evaluation.
9. The uniforms/CSD chosen must adhere to the current district policies and procedures.
10. All parents/guardians shall be given timely notification of the deadline for completing and submitting a CSD Waiver.
11. Any parent/guardian requesting a CSD Waiver must do so within the publicized deadline and attend a meeting with school principal or designee as part of the waiver request.
12. CSD Waivers requested beyond the designated deadline will not be accepted except for special circumstances/compelling reasons where the school is not able to provide a remedy or where the school had not given timely notification to the parent/guardian.
13. When all procedures are competed and a uniform/CSD program has been approved by the parents/guardians for implementation, all students are expected to participate in the program except for those students on waivers.
14. Students who have a signed waiver will receive a ¬ëopt out' card and be required to supply the opt out card when requested during the school day or at school events.
15. School will maintain an ¬ëopt out' waiver roster for all students with active waiver forms.
16. Students who participate in recognized youth organizations shall be allowed to wear organization uniforms. (Education Cod e 351 83)
17. Schools must participate in an evaluation at the end of the first and third years of participation in a uniform/CSD program which may include such factors as:
  1. Self-concept
  2. Discipline
  3. Attendance
  4. Attitude toward school
  5. Morale
  6. Peer relationships
  7. Parent/guardian concerns
  8. Suspension referrals
  9. Behavior referrals
  10. Other factors as related to the stated objective

**School Process**

1. The principal indicates in writing to the Assistant Superintendent of Secondary or Elementary Curriculum and Instruction that the school is interested in beginning the process.
2. The school receives information explaining all requirements and processes.
3. The principal meets with staff and parent groups including school site council to discuss the concept of uniforms/CSD. A meeting must also be conducted in other language(s) appropriate for the school community. A subcommittee of these groups will be formed to complete the following:
  1. Obtain uniform/CSD samples and cost information from a variety of companies.
  2. Explore possible ways to obtain financial assistance for families in need.
  3. Send written communication to all parents/guardians, staff, and students inviting them to a general information meeting on uniforms/CSD. ​​​​​​​
4. Hold school information meeting(s) to: ​​​​​​​
  1. Provide opportunities for questions, discussions, input.
  2. Display sample uniforms and costs.
  3. Discuss process and time line for decision-making and voting.
  4. Review district parameters and expectations.
  5. Conduct a survey of those present. (If there is a lack of interest and support at this point, and/or complications that cannot be resolved, the school should not consider participating in the program at this time.) ​​​​​​​
5. Based on an analysis of #3 and #4 above, the school determines whether or not to continue.
6. Conduct the vote only once during the school year by distributing ballots to all parents/guardians by family.
7. Tabulate results of balloting.
8. Publicize the results to the school community.
9. Forward the completed "Request to Participate in School Uniform Program" with supporting information to the appropriate assistant superintendent for approval.
10. School is to await Board approval.
11. Provide written information regarding the process and semester deadlines for parents/guardians to request CSD waivers. School must notify families and provide for a 6-month implementation timeline.
12. Principal communicates in writing to parents/guardians and students explaining the purchase options, waiver option and implementation guidelines and financial resources available immediately following Board approval and at least six month prior to implementing CDS/uniforms.

**Evaluating Whether to Continue, Rescind, or Modify**

In order to discontinue or modify school uniforms/common student dress (CSD), all schools must adhere to the following school parameters, guidelines, and process.

**Parameters**

1. The principal or designee shall give parents/guardians at least six month's notice before a school uniform/common student dress policy is rescinded or modified.
2. The principal must document, in writing, that the parents/guardians, community members, and staff have had sufficient opportunities to study and address all the related issues.

**Guidelines**

1. Both the decision to rescind and the decision to modify will be school-based.
2. Surveys designed for use in determining the interest of staff, parents/guardians and students for uniforms/CSD must be prepared in the languages appropriate for the school community.
3. Schools will have district support regarding the conducting of procedures and the evaluation process.
4. Changes to CSD must adhere to the current district policies and procedures.

**School Process**

1. The principal indicates in writing to the Superintendent or designee that the school is interested in beginning the process.
2. The school receives information explaining all requirements and processes.
3. The principal or designee meets with staff and parent groups, including school site council, to discuss the evaluation process and possible changes to the school site's CSD.
4. The process for evaluation of CSD may include the following: ​​​​​​​
  1. Appointment of a school CSD evaluation committee consisting of parents/guardians, staff and principal or designee
  2. Parent/guardian survey of incoming and returning students
  3. Parent/community meetings
  4. Newsletter articles
  5. Census data (number of students in CSD compliance on any given day)
  6. Staff input
  7. Percent of waivers
  8. Effect on the learning environment
  9. Effect on student academic performance and behavior ​​​​​​​
5. Anecdotal information
6. The principal or designee conducts school information meeting(s) to: ​​​​​​​
  1. Provide opportunities for questions, discussions, and input
  2. Discuss process and timeline for decision-making
  3. Review district parameters and expectations ​​​​​​​
7. In order for the Board to approve any school site recommendations, the school must document that the guidelines and school process were followed. The final decision on whether or not to continue, rescind, or modify common student dress should be based on but not be limited to the following: ​​​​​​​
  1. Percentage of students who are on waivers
  2. Effect on student performance and behavior
  3. Effect on the learning environment
  4. Results of the parent surveys
  5. Input from staff ​​​​​​​
8. The principal or designee reports the recommendation with supporting information to the Superintendent/designee.
9. The principal or designee publicizes the results of the decision to the school community.
